FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Paraborg require Administrator permissions?

By default, the bot role is granted Administrator permissions. However, the permission can be revoked should you not want it. However the bot should be granted appropriate permissions depending on the module / package you plan to use!

Delay in sending Global-Chat messages?

Currently, its Paraborg's second layer of protection from Global Chat overload.

Can the prefix of Paraborg be changed?

Yes! With the use of !prefix (New Prefix).

Where do I get help for Paraborg?

You may use !help or !help (Spefic Command) if you wish to learn the usage and purpose of existing commands! You may also check out our tutorial page or our documentations to further read up on Paraborg!

Which Programming Language is being used to develop Paraborg?

Paraborg is developed using C#, and is currently using the NET.core, an open source web framework.

What is the difference between Guild-Admin and Bot-Admin commands?

Guild-Admin commands may be run by any user with Administrator permission, or any user who is !addmod (ed) within the specific guild. Bot-Admin commands are different in the sense that they can only be run by users who are whitelisted in Paraborg's whitelist. Usually, these users are staffs of our official server, Our Globe Now (OGN)

What happens if I forget the Prefix of Paraborg in my designated server?

Pinging / Mentioning the bot overrides all prefixes! You can then change the bot's prefix by (Bot Mention) prefix (New Prefix)

Why is the bot named Paraborg? Who came up with that name?

"Para" is short for the word "Paragon", which means being perfect at every aspect of things, and short for "Paramount", which suggests Supermacy and Superiority. Paraborg by no means is a perfect bot, but the bot definitely is made based on our user's needs, and we are constantly adding user suggested features to make the bot as versatile as possible. "Para" helps highlight the versatility of Paraborg. "Borg" originates from the word "Cyborg", a synonym for "Bot". "Borg" is used over "Bot" as bot is too generic and mainstream of a term. The name is founded by me and NikkaNC#6724, me having come up with "Para" and him coming up with "Borg".
